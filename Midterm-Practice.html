# Naive 
naive_forecast <- naive(ausbeer, 10)

accuracy (naive_forecast)
##                     ME     RMSE      MAE       MPE    MAPE    MASE       ACF1
## Training set 0.4147465 67.67304 56.76498 -1.083692 13.3973 3.66005 -0.2214297
res_naive <- residuals(naive_forecast)
plot(res_naive)

hist(res_naive)


####Summary of Naive Forecasting Method: The naive method sets the forecast for the next period equal to the last actual observation. Works reasonably well for random walk processes or time series with no clear trend or seasonality, but performs poorly for series with trends or seasonality.The forecast for all future periods (one year ahead) is the same as the last observed value. Between 1990, the residuals were greater than any previous years, this could be because of a boom in beer popularity that caused the Australian production to lag behind.This histogram shows more frequency in the negative residuals meaning that it was more common for them to over-produce beer.

lines(MA9_forecast, col="green")

#### Observations: A moving averages plot is a way to smooth out a time series and reveal underlying trends or patterns by reducing noise. As the order gets larger, the line moves closer to an average/smoothing, decreasing the "noise".
